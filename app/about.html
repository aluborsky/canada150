About Us

Our Canada, Our Stories – Notre Canada, Nos Histories: Our Canada 150 Project is a national, not-for-profit collaborative campaign to celebrate Canada's 150th birthday in 2017 by encouraging Canadians of all ages and backgrounds to record their short and full-length personal, family and community histories.

Founded by Harry van Bommel on July 1, 1997 and coordinated by Legacies Inc., the project is the largest history-gathering project in Canada.

Mission Statement

We will engage Canadians in a celebration of their past and future by encouraging them to record the stories of their lives, their families and their communities for the benefit of future generations. We will make it enjoyable and straightforward for these irreplaceable memories to be documented and then gathered in an unprecedented collection, mostly online and available to researchers and the public in perpetuity..

Values

1) Collaboration 2) Inclusiveness 3) Transparency 4) Flexibility 5) Technological and Archival Rigour

Vision

Our project will engage Canadians from coast to coast to coast in a myriad of national and grassroots programs to record their memories and their histories, both to celebrate and record the past and present, while creating an unparalleled legacy resource for the future.

To provide flexibility and reliable infrastructure for this celebration, our project is helping to create rock-solid technology and has integrated state-of-the-art archival principles into the design of our programs. The resulting database will be robust, scalable, adaptable, easy to update and a breeze to access. Appropriate measures will safeguard privacy and copyright.

Goals

 To retain our wealth of oral, written and visual history for generations to come in digital repositories available to everyone worldwide.

 To provide a family legacy.

 To improve understanding within families and communities through the mutual exchange of stories.

 To enhance Canadian unity through a sense of national pride and a more profound celebration of the contributions of Canadians from diverse backgrounds.

Objectives

 To engage millions of Canadians in a consideration of the past, present, and future and to improve understanding within families and communities.

 To be memorable beyond 2017 through a rich resource of primary materials, both oral and written, available in perpetuity.

Specifically Canada 150 will collect short and full-length histories through:

 Memories [uploads of photos and one-to-two paragraph stories about a specific event or family]

 One-of-a-Kind [unpublished collections of letters, journals, diaries, films, scrapbooks]

 Published [books, films, songs, plays, websites, multimedia platforms]

Types of Stories

If something is important in the lives of people, its story should be recorded. Stories may be:

 An individual's stories.

 Neighbourhood, community, municipality, county and regional histories.

 Stories and histories from faith communities and their places of worship.

 Histories of social clubs, associations, universities and colleges, arts and sports groups, educational institutions and more.

 Corporate histories.

Scope

The project will reach out to all Canadians (both inside and outside of Canada) and encourage them to use all mediums available to them. Our goal remains to collect and preserve these stories so the final products must be either in a published format or a digital one. There will be no creative limits to the submissions as Canadians are a wonderfully diverse and creative population.

Target Audiences

As Canada’s largest history gathering project ever, we must be inclusive to fulfill our scope. Special emphasis is needed to reach the broadest audience possible including:

 youth, elders, boomers, aboriginal groups, veterans, community groups, corporations, women's groups, risk groups, as many others as we can bring together.

Funding

Our project will be funded in two ways:

 Canadians already are paying for their own computers, software, print-on-demand books, video recorders, scrapbooks, CDs, websites, etc. We will offer them encouragement, “how to” materials, ways to locate local resources that can help them, and a place to put these records, in perpetuity, as our gifts to Canada on its 150th birthday.

 Governments (all levels), granting agencies (e.g., foundations), and corporations will be asked to provide funding to co-ordinate this largest history-gathering project in Canadian history. As well they may help fund local and national programs that make special efforts to reach those Canadians who are less likely to record their stories without help and provide some of the equipment, such as digital scanners, to help communities record their local photos, films and documents in perpetuity.
