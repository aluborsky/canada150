Getting Started

Our project officially started on July 1, 2012 - giving us less than 3 years to help Canadians collect, share and preserve their memories and stories. Some ways to get started:

 You can read the FREE online book Recording Your Life and Family Stories. You can print off any of the pages you like. That means you can start recording your stories immediately.

 You can check the links page for websites you may find very helpful. Enjoy your learning as you become an even greater expert on your family and community histories.

 You can learn more about the Canada 150 project and our partners.

 You can check out our latest news and sign up for the RSS feed which automatically tells your email account when we have added a new entry.

 All other digital materials will be preserved at our partner’s site Our Digital World, i.e., digital copies of “one-of-a-kind” materials, unpublished materials, photos, videos, art, and music. We are now ready to collect stories and photos. Go to our collection page to upload your stories. Later in 2015-2016 we will begin to collect other digital records.

 You can become a local “Ambassador” by telling your family and friends about Canada 150 and encouraging them to start recording their life and family stories.

 If you are a professional writer, editor, printer, publisher, film maker, multi-media producer, photographer, videographer, musician, and/or artist, you can begin to help Canadians record their life and family stories. Use the free online book above as a starting point. Offer people your professional help for a reasonable fee and share in their excitement as they prepare materials for their descendants and for their country. Don’t forget to record your own stories for the project as well!
