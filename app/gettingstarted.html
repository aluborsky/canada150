Getting Started

Our project officially started on July 1. Some ways to get started:

 You can start by writing a short 2-3 sentence story right now! Go to our digital collection at canada150.org and write a short memory or story about your life, your parents or grandparents, or your favourite memory as a child.

 Check out our 24-week project idea here.

 You can get our FREE online book Recording Your Life and Family Stories. It will help you get started right away in recording your stories.

 You can check the links page for websites you may find very helpful. Enjoy your learning as you become an even greater expert on your family and community histories.

 You can keep up-to-date following us on Facebook and Twitter.

 All other digital materials will be preserved at our partner’s site Our Digital World. We are now ready to collect stories and photos. Go to our collection page to upload your stories. Later in 2015-2016 we will begin to collect other digital records such as scanned documents, letters, journals, diaries, and annotated scrapbooks and photo albums.

 You can become a local “Ambassador” by telling your family and friends about our project and encouraging them to start recording their life and family stories.

 If you are a professional writer, editor, printer, publisher, filmmaker, multi-media producer, photographer, videographer, musician, and/or artist, you can begin to help Canadians record their life and family stories. Use the free eBook as a starting point. Offer people your professional help for a reasonable fee and share in their excitement as they prepare materials for their descendants and for their country. Don’t forget to record your own stories for the project as well! Check out our “For Writers and Editors” handout.
