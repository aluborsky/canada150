Partners

The following organizations are partners of the Canada 150 project. They have committed to creating their own unique projects to help Canadians record their short and full-length family and community histories as well as municipal and corporate histories. Check out their websites to learn more about these organizations.

The digital collection will be preserved by:

Our Digital World will host and maintain our extensive digital collection of stories, photos and videos. We recommend their services to our partners who wish to have their own collection.

Founding Partners

Canada's History is Canada’s premier history site for students, teachers, history buffs, history professors and anyone interested in learning about, or participating in, the history of Canada. It has a robust website chalk full of resources, as well a magazine for adults and one for children. They work with the Governor General of Canada to honour our best history teachers annually. Canada’s History is a fundamental partner in Canada 150 and has been for many years.

Canadian Science & Technology Museums Corporation is responsible for preserving and protecting Canada’s scientific and technological heritage and promoting and sharing knowledge about that heritage. The Corporation and its three Museums — the Canada Agriculture Museum, the Canada Aviation and Space Museum, and the Canada Science and Technology Museum — tell the stories of Canadian ingenuity and achievement in science and technology, and demonstrate how these accomplishments have contributed to the building of our country.

The Canadian Abilities Foundation provides a forum for the exchange of information to promote an inclusive society provide inspiration and opportunity for people with disabilities.

Bullseye Canada publishes The Canadian Pop Music Encyclopedia, containing discographies and biographies for more than 2,300 of Canada's most celebrated major label and independent music acts spanning 60 years of Canadian popular music with a focus on Pop, Rock, Folk and Singer-Songwriter genres.

Canadian Editors Association promotes professional editing and their 1,600 members can help Canadians edit their family and organizational histories.

The Canadian Expat Network is the leading Canadian expat site for Canadians living abroad. CEN provides bi-weekly e-newsletters, regular stories of interest, expat webinars, calendar of events and regularly promotes regional Canadian Expat groups.

The Canadian Foundation for Animal-Assisted Support Services improves the health and quality of life of people with physical, emotional, and social challenges through partnerships with companion and service animals. Their project, “The Animal in War” will highlight the invaluable role of animals in our military.

Canadian Mosaic Tim Van Horn will cross the Canadian landscape photographing thousands of Canadians. This project attempts to unite the country, a people in one single all encompassing, euphoric visual. On Canada Day 2017, a larger-than-life 30 x 100 foot Canadian flag mosaic will be unveiled at a major Canadian museum. This living tapestry of life, will play an instrumental role in bringing together the people of Canada.

Caregiver Omnimedia reaches an audience of 100,000s of family caregivers across Canada, each of whom has a story.

Compass Digital Media a BC-based videography company that wants to help Canadians record their stories on videos.

Claudia Cornwall [Simon Fraser University Family Memoir Instructor] a continuing education program to help students learn the skills in writing memoirs.

Federated Women’s Institutes of Ontario is a “not-for-profit charitable organization with affiliations around the world, working with and for women in rural and small town Ontario. Celebrating their 115th anniversary in 2012, the WI has been capturing and preserving local community history for more than 80 years. Their Tweedsmuir Community History Collections have been recognized as important resources for studying Ontario history at the local level.

Heritage Memoirs produces one-of-a-kind life stories, memoirs, personal histories, family histories and corporate histories from recorded interviews.

imagiNATION 150 is based in Calgary and wants to encourage Canadians to celebrate our country’s 150th birthday by offering Canada a gift, great or small, that kindles our collective spirit and illuminates the Canada we could be. Canada 150 is a proud participant as we believe that stories make an excellent birthday gift!

Kingston Frontenac Public Library began in 1812. While the city's first library collections were provided by private citizens, anyone could borrow books for a small fee. What better year to celebrate this library’s commitment to it patrons’s stories than during the War of 1812 activities.

Partners

Canadian Association of Community Television Users CACTUS was created to educate consumers and to improve access by Canadians to two specific media services: Local media, especially video and television programming and media production training and equipment at the local level, especially video and television.

CARP Canada is a national, non-partisan, non-profit organization committed to advocating for a New Vision of Aging for Canada, promoting social change that will bring financial security, equitable access to health care and freedom from age discrimination.

The Canadian Race Relations Foundation is Canada’s leading agency dedicated to the elimination of racism and the promotion of harmonious race relations.

IOTAD is a non-profit organization committed to traveling and discovering what makes us human. This always leads to the visual storytellers out there…whether its a painter capturing the landscape on canvas or a potter creating from raw elements…we always come away with a greater sense of who we are. Without these "stories" and the "storytellers" we would be void of culture. For 2017 we will journey into and build Canada’s Artography. We are "getting to know Canada, one artist at a time".

The Jewish Genealogical Society of Toronto's mission is to stimulate interest in the pursuit of Jewish genealogical research and facilitate that research locally and internationally.

Legacies is a Canadian not-for-profit organization committed to helping Canadians record their family, community and organizational histories. They have served as the administration of the Canada 150 project since 1999.

The Manitoba Social Science Teachers’ Association is a non-profit organizations run by a volunteer team of leaders to ensure that the Social Sciences continue to be a valuable and significant part of Kindergarten-to-Grade 12 in Manitoba for every student.

Moorshead Magazines: publishers of Family Chronicle (the ‘how to’ magazine for tracing your ancestors), Internet Genealogy (the magazine to help you use the Internet for family history research), and History Magazine (provides interesting and thought-provoking accounts of key events in global history).

The Ontario Library Association is the oldest and largest library association in Canada with more than 5,000 members in public libraries, schools, and corporations.
